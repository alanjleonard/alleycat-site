Alleycat

An alleycat is an unsanctioned bike race with multiple checkpoints and no set route. It's like a scavenger hunt for locations in Hamilton. It's history starts in Toronto in the '80s and has since expanded world-wide. Originally it was a way for bike messengers to see who the fastest was. The race models the job of a bike messenger: In a city, no set route, several locations. The Steel City Alleycat is a small part of a much bigger history of urban cycling. There's a documentary on YouTube called First Cats that talks about it's chaotic origins.

Free Entry

This will always be a free ride. I'm never going to ask you for your money.

All are welcome

Traditionally, alleycat bike races are done on fixed gear bikes—since this was a prefered bike of messengers, but I'm not going to police what you ride. In the past we've had e-bikes, multiple tandem bikes, kid-trailers, recumbent bikes, SoBis, dogs, big teams, elite level bikes, and dumpster-ready-shit-boxes. Riders from under one year old to over 70 years old.
